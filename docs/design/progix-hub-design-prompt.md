You are a senior product designer and UI/UX professional. Deliver polished, production-grade screens that respect HMI principles, accessibility, and visual hierarchy. Output designs only — no code.

## Product

progixHub is the internal home base for every Progix project. A team member creates a project, then attaches its four “surfaces” by pasting links — its Notion page, its Slack channel, its GitHub repo — and keeps the project’s environment variables (secured) and documents in one place. It is to whole projects what GitHub is to a single repo: a calm, trustworthy registry the team opens to find everything about a project in one glance. The emotional register is **focused, secure, and quietly powerful** — a tool insiders rely on every day, not a flashy marketing surface. It is bilingual (English + French): every screen must work in both languages, so leave room for longer French strings.

## Users & primary journeys

- **Founder / leadership** → open progixHub and see the whole portfolio of projects, their status at a glance, and jump into any one.
- **Project manager** → open a project and instantly reach its Notion, Slack, GitHub, documents, and env vars — all current, all one click away.
- **Developer / agent** → find a project’s environment variables, reveal the one they need (a deliberate, secure gesture), and copy it — without asking anyone.
- **External client** (separate, minimal surface) → open a link and leave feedback on their project, with no account and no access to anything else.

## Surfaces to design

- **Admin dashboard (web, authenticated)** — the core product. Key views:
  - **Sign in** — a small, secure, invite-only screen: “Continue with GitHub” and “Email me a magic link.”
  - **Projects list** — the portfolio: every project as a card or row, with name, a status indicator, and quick links to its surfaces. Needs a clear “New project” entry and search/filter. Design the empty state (no projects yet).
  - **Create / edit project** — name the project and paste its Notion, Slack, and GitHub links (with gentle validation and link previews). Effortless; this is the signature gesture.
  - **Project detail** — the heart of the app. A header with the project name and the four surface links as prominent, recognizable shortcuts. Tabbed or sectioned body for: **Env vars**, **Documents**, **Feedback**.
  - **Env vars** — a table of key/value pairs where values are **masked by default** and revealed only by a deliberate per-row action (with a subtle “revealed” state and a copy affordance). This must _feel_ secure and intentional — the visual centerpiece of trust in the product. Design add/edit and the empty state.
  - **Documents** — three kinds in one place: uploaded files (with type/size and download), external links (Figma, Drive, design ZIPs), and rich-text notes written in-app. Design upload, list, and empty states.
- **Client feedback page (web, external, link-gated)** — a single, simple, on-brand page where a client reads a short project context line and submits feedback (rating + message). Confirmation state included. No navigation into the rest of the app.

## Visual direction

- **Personality:** **designer’s call — propose the direction.** Two archetypes to react to (pick one or blend, and show your reasoning in the design):
  - _Developer-tool dark_ — dense, calm dark UI, monospace accents, keyboard-friendly, reads like GitHub/Linear. Leans into the “like GitHub, for projects” framing.
  - _Clean SaaS dashboard_ — light, airy, generous whitespace, approachable, reads like Vercel/Notion.
    Whichever you choose, optimize for **legibility, scannability, and a sense of security** — this is a daily-driver internal tool.
- **Brand:** no logo or fixed palette yet — you have freedom to propose a simple wordmark and a restrained palette. Keep it neutral and professional; avoid loud, consumer-marketing color. (Brand assets are an open question; treat your proposal as a starting point, not a mandate.)
- **References (attached as images):** _attach 3–6 Pinterest / Behance / Dribbble picks and write one line per reference on what to borrow — the visual treatment, not the function (e.g. “this card density,” “this masked-secret row pattern,” “this sign-in calm”)._

## Expected result & quality bar

- Every screen ships its real states: **empty, loading, error, success** — especially the projects list (no projects), env vars (none yet, and the revealed/hidden states), documents (none yet), and the feedback confirmation.
- The env-vars masked/reveal interaction must read as deliberate and trustworthy — this is the product’s signature moment.
- Bilingual-ready: layouts tolerate longer French text without breaking.
- Accessible: clear contrast, visible focus states, labelled controls, keyboard-friendly navigation.
- Consistency across surfaces: a real, reusable design system (typography scale, spacing, components), not one-off screens.

## Out of scope for this pass

- Live data from Notion/GitHub/Slack — surfaces are pasted links, not synced; do not design dashboards that mirror those tools.
- Task/kanban/sprint management — progixHub links to GitHub/Notion for that; do not design a project-management board.
- Any client-facing surface beyond the single feedback page.
- Encryption/vault administration screens — env vars are login-gated for this version.
